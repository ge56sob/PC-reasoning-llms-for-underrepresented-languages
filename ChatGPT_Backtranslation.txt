Initially, Turbo does not know where any monster is, but he knows that there is exactly one monster in every row except the first and last rows, and that each column contains at most one monster.

Turbo makes a sequence of attempts to travel from the first row to the last row. In each attempt, he starts at any cell in the first row and repeatedly moves to an edge-adjacent cell. (He may revisit previously visited cells.) If he enters a cell containing a monster, the attempt immediately ends, and he is returned to the first row to begin a new attempt. The monsters do not move, and Turbo remembers whether each cell he has visited contains a monster or not. If he reaches any cell in the last row, the attempt ends and the game is won.

Determine the smallest value of n such that Turbo has a strategy guaranteeing that he reaches the last row on or before the n-th attempt, regardless of the placement of the monsters.

Solution

Let the board have

R=2024 rows,
C=2023 columns.

There are 2022=R−2 monsters, one in each interior row, and each column contains at most one monster.

Key observation

Each failed attempt reveals the location of exactly one previously unknown monster, because the attempt ends immediately upon stepping onto a monster.

Since every interior row has exactly one monster, there are exactly 2022 monsters altogether.

Upper bound

A simple strategy is:

explore cautiously downward,
whenever a monster is encountered, remember its location,
on later attempts never step on that monster again.

After at most 2022 failures, every monster location is known.

Since each interior row has exactly one blocked cell and every other cell is safe, Turbo can then plan an entirely safe path from the first row to the last.

Thus victory is guaranteed on the next attempt.

Hence

n≤2022+1=2023.
Lower bound

Now suppose an adversary chooses the monster positions adaptively, consistent with everything Turbo has seen.

Before Turbo has discovered all 2022 monsters, there is still some interior row whose monster position is unknown.

Whenever Turbo first enters such a row through a previously untested cell, the adversary can declare that very cell to contain the monster (while keeping all constraints satisfied, since each column has been used at most once).

Therefore every attempt before all 2022 monsters have been identified can be forced to fail.

So Turbo cannot guarantee success before attempt

2022+1=2023.

Since the upper and lower bounds agree,

n=2023.
